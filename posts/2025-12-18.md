### The Hidden Dangers of Unreliable Dashboards in Decision-Making

Unreliable dashboards often mislead decision-makers, creating a significant risk for organizations. When engineers build analytics tools without sufficient input from leaders, crucial metrics can be misrepresented. This misalignment leads to decision-making based on inaccurate data. Non-technical leaders may prioritize projects or allocate resources based on flawed interpretations, ultimately steering efforts away from what truly matters for the business.

Vendors can also exacerbate this problem by providing generic solutions that fail to align with specific organizational needs. When teams implement these tools without adequate customization, the insights become superficial and unreliable. This disconnect stifles meaningful collaboration and weakens trust between technical and non-technical stakeholders.
